Jollof Rice
Ingredients
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 3 plum tomatoes
  • 2 white or red onions
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper
  • ½ cup (118 ml) of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon (2.4 g) of curry powder
  • ½ teaspoon (0.5 g) of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon (2.4 g) of white pepper
  • 2 cubes of chicken bouillon
  • 1 cup (237 ml) of chicken stock
  • 2 cups (400 g) of long-grain, parboiled rice (like Golden Sella basmati)
  • Salt to taste
    • Servings: 4
    Steps: It is key to use parboiled rice so that you don't end up with mushy rice in the end. When cooking the tomato stew, you can tell when it is done because the oil will rise to the top. It will be a deep red color and the bitter taste of the tomatoes has been cooked out. Reserve 1/4th of the tomato mixture and add it at the end to help prevent the rice from burning. Also, it gives the rice that beautiful red color we all know and love. Cover the top of the pot with plastic wrap before putting the lid on to help keep all the steam in the pot.
    Vegetable Soup
    Ingredients
  • Big bowl Waterleaves
  • Big bowl ugu leaves (fluted pumpkin)
  • 1KG or 35 Ounces of Goat meat
  • 1 cup crayfish
  • 6 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 400ml Palm oil
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons Ground Ofor
    • Servings: 5
    Steps: Thinly the slice the waterleaves and fluted pumpkin (ugu leaves) Wash the ugu leaves before slicing it. Slice the water leaves then wash it. Allow them to strain seperatrly using a sieve to remove excess water Clean and wash the roasted fish, pound your crayfish/peppers. Season the meat and let it cook till it becomes tender. Add the washed roasted fish to the boiling meat, let them boil together till the water dries. To the meat/fish on heat, add palm oil (400ml) The vegetables should be added, the water leaves should come first. After adding all the water leaves, allow it to simmer for 2-4 minutes while you stir occasionally. Keep stirring. Add the pounded crayfish and peppers. Add salt to your taste. Ugu leaves should follow at this point (fluted pumpkin) Stir in 2 tablespoons of ofor (used as a thickener). Stir properly so it doesn't form crumbs. Stir and allow the soup to simmer for 2 minutes, stir and serve.
    Yam and Egg sauce
    Ingredients
  • Tuber of yam
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ tsp salt plus 1 tsp for yam
  • 1 Seasoning cube
  • ½ tsp curry
  • Cray fish powered or prefered
    • Servings: 3 avg
    Steps: Slice yam into about 1-inch size and peel the skin off.Rinse in clean water till water runs clear and no residue in water. Put yam in a pan, add enough water to cover it, add salt and place on medium heat and cook till soft. Test with a fork, if it goes through, then the yam should be soft enough. Drain water from yam if you want. Break eggs into a shallow bowl, whisk till egg white and egg yolk are well combined, add salt and whisk to combine then set it aside Place a frying pan on a medium/low heat, add of vegetable oil, heat for about 2-3 minutes Add chopped peppers and onions (this should sizzle) and cook for about 4-5 minutes or till oil floats on the sauce, add salt, seasoning, curry and thyme. Stir to combine ingredient with sauce and also avoid sauce sticking to the pan.
    Spaghetti and Beans
    Ingredients
  • 1 packet of spaghetti
  • ½ cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of beans
  • Tomatoes (grinded)
  • Pepper (grinded)
  • Onions (grinded)
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Spices
  • Salt to taste
  • Meat or fish (optional)
    • Servings: 4
    Steps: STEP 1: Pick out the dirt and unwanted particles from the beans. Wash the beans when you are done picking. STEP 2: Over medium heat, place the cooking pot, add water, and add the beans. Cook for 40 minutes, or until very soft. STEP 3: When the beans is cooked, use the sieve to drain the water and keep the beans aside. STEP 4: Over medium heat, place another pot and add water. Allow to boil. STEP 5: Once the water is boiling, add the spaghetti and cook until half-done. Use the sieve to drain the water. STEP 6: Then put the frying pan on the cooker and add vegetable oil and little sliced onions to it. Allow to fry for 3 minutes. STEP 7: Slice your onions, tomatoes, and pepper and add them to the frying pan. Allow 10 minutes to fry. STEP 8: When well fried, add 2 cups of water and add the fish or meat to it. (Remember, adding the fish or meat is optional). Allow the meat or fish to cook for 30 minutes. STEP 9: When the meat or fish is cooked and very soft, add the boiled beans and spaghetti to the cooking pot. Add your seasonings and salt to taste. Allow to cook for 15 minutes. STEP 10: Your Beans and Spaghetti is ready to be served.
